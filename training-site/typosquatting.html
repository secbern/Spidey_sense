How to read a domain (left to right) In the address bar, read left to right. After https:// you see the host: the part that identifies the site. The main site name is the word or phrase right before the last dot and the ending (like .com or .org). For example, in www.paypal.com the main name is paypal; www is a subdomain (a prefix). Scammers often put a brand name in the subdomain so the URL looks real, but the part right before .com or .xyz is the real owner — read that part carefully.
Typosquatting – what fake domains often look like (defanged examples) Scammers use lookalike spelling or extra words. Examples that are not real links: paypa1[.]com (number 1 instead of letter L), amaz0n[-]security[.]com (zero instead of O, plus “security”), g00gle[.]com (zeros instead of O), micros0ft[.]support[.]xyz (zero and a sketchy .xyz), apple[.]com[-]login[.]evil[.]xyz (brand in subdomain; real domain is evil[.]xyz). When in doubt, type the real site’s address yourself.
