Training demo: Impersonation / urgent money request. Back to list | Español
In this scenario (impersonation): Scammers pretend to be family, your boss, or the IRS and create urgency ("right now," "don't tell anyone"). Real family or employers won't ask for gift card codes or wire transfers as the only option. Verify by calling the person on a number you already have (not one in the message) or in person.

Urgent – Need your help

From: "Grandma Sarah" <grandma.sarah.urgent@gmail.com>

Hi honey, this is Grandma. I'm in trouble – I had an accident and I'm in the hospital but my phone broke. I'm borrowing a nurse's tablet.

I need $3,500 right away for the hospital. They won't release me until I pay. Please don't tell your mom and dad, they would worry too much. Can you get gift cards (Apple or Google Play) and send me the codes? Or wire money to this account?

Please reply as soon as you see this. I love you.

– Grandma

Reply to this email or send codes to: urgent.family.help@gmail.com
