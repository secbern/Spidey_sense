Welcome

This site helps you practice spotting online fraud in a safe environment. Each scenario shows what common scams often look like—fake logins, prize claims, tech support popups, and more—so you can learn what to watch for without any risk.

We built it especially for older adults and non‑native English speakers, but anyone can use it. You can also test the Spidey Sense browser extension here. Choose a scenario below to get started.

Fraud scenario demos

Typosquatting & reading URLs

How to read a web address left to right and recognize fake domains (e.g. paypa1[.]com, amaz0n[-]security[.]com).

Phishing emails

Clean examples of fake “account alert” and “delivery” emails, plus how to evaluate the true sender.

Fake login (phishing)

A page that looks like a real bank or service login to steal your username and password.

Prize or lottery scam

"You've won!" – asks for fees or personal details to claim a fake prize.

Tech support scam

Fake virus or "Microsoft" popup urging you to call a number or install software.

Fake shopping / too-good deal

Unrealistic discounts or products that never arrive; steals payment or personal info.

Impersonation (urgent money request)

Pretends to be family, boss, or authority and asks for money or gift cards urgently.

Fake charity

Uses a real disaster or cause to collect donations that never go to victims.
